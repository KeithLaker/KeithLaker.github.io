Data Warehouse Use Case Patterns

Modern Data Warehouse



A modern data warehouse collects data from a wide variety of sources, both internal or external. It optimizes the data for retrieval for business purposes. The data is usually structured, often from relational databases, but it can be unstructured too pulled from "big data" sources such as Internet of Things devices etc.

Autonomous Data Warehouse makes it easy to integrate and secure data from many sources and then generate richer, smarter, faster business insights...




Spread-Marts



A spread-mart is used by individual teams or individual departments or groups and is intentionally limited in scope because it focuses on a clearly defined subset of data contained withing spreadsheets. Spreadsheets were never designed to be a secure, robust, data sharing or collaboration tool. Therefore, spread-marts create all sorts of problems for users.

Fortunately, Autonomous Database makes it easy to move spread-marts to a safe, secure always-on cloud...




Data Lake



A data lake is a place to store your structured and unstructured data, as well as a method for organizing large volumes of highly diverse data from diverse sources.

An Autonomous Data Lake is able to ingest data very quickly and prepare it on the fly so teams can analyze and evaluate new data streams using a wide range of smart analytics - machine learning, graph, spatial, semantic analytics...




Data Discovery Sandboxes



A data discovery sandbox is generally meant as a non-operational environment where business analysts and data scientists can test ideas, manipulate data and model what-if scenarios.

It has a finite life expectancy and when timer runs out the sandbox is deleted. The associated discoveries are then either incorporated into the warehouse or simply abandoned.

The driver is to use a 'fail-fast" approach built around Autonomous Database and its wide range of in-database smart analytics - machine learning, graph, spatial, semantic analytics...
