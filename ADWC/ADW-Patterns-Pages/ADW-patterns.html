Data Warehouse Use Case Patterns

Modern Data Warehouse

Spread-Marts

Data Lake

Data Discovery Sandboxes
 

A modern data warehouse collects data from a wide variety of sources, both internal or external. It optimizes the data for retrieval for business purposes. The data is usually structured, often from relational databases, but it can be unstructured too pulled from "big data" sources such as Internet of Things devices etc.

Primarily, the data warehouse is designed to gather business insights and allows businesses to integrate their data, manage it, and analyze it at many levels.




A spread-mart is used by individual teams or individual departments or groups and is intentionally limited in scope because it focuses on a clearly defined subset of data that is relevant to the main users need right now versus the data that may already exist within the organization.











A data lake is a place to store your structured and unstructured data, as well as a method for organizing large volumes of highly diverse data from diverse sources.

The data lake tends to ingest data very quickly and prepare it later, on the fly, as people access it..











A data discovery sandbox is generally meant as a non-operational environment where business analysts and data scientists can test ideas, manipulate data and model what-if scenarios.

It has a finite life expectancy and when timer runs out the sandbox is deleted. The associated discoveries are then either incorporated into the enterprise warehouse/data mart or simply abandoned.

The primary driver from an organisational perspective is to use a 'fail-fast" approach.
